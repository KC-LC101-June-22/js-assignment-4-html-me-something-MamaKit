Animals

Animals the world over are believed to have various attributes and powers. These attributes can vary greatly depending on culture & history. For example: In the United States, a black cat crossing your path is considered unlucky. However, in England, a black cat crossing your path is good luck, while a white cat is concerned incredibly unlucky. Comparing both these attributions with Ancient Egypt, where cats were worshipped as gods, shows that great variations in correspondence between animals and their attirbutions & powers. For my purposes, I will be including my own personal correspondences.

Colors

Colors have always elicited a rather visceral reaction from us. Mostly, you either love a given color, or you HATE it with the fiery passion of 1,000,000,000 supernovas. Color theory takes these reactions & responses, & defines the average response to different colors. Magically speaking, each of us does the same thing. When you have to make a big presentation, do you wear a color you love for a confidence boost, or a color that you hate? That's literally a low-key spell. So, let's jump into some of the basic color correspondences.

Deities

I have always been drawn to stories of gods, heroes, & villains, regardless of the culture. Ancient Egyptian, Greek, First Nations, Scottish, Irish, Norse. All hold appeal. I have worked with deities from all the aforementioned pantheons, except First Nations pantheons. There are two reasons for this.

  1. Despite family history on both sides claiming Cherokee blood, I cannot find any records to support it. Without a legitimate claim to the heritage, trying to worship the gods of a closed pantheon is both disrespectful & stupid.
  2. I've never felt called to any of the entities I discovered in the course of my research.

Below I have listed the primary deities that I have previously &/or currently work with &/or worship.

Apollo

I started working with Apollo while I was in high school. One of our humanities classes focused on Greco-Roman myths, & the book we used listed Apollo as a healing god. I've always been clumsy. So, having to literally run down hallways & up-&-down stairs carrying a backpack that weighed at least much as (& often more than) me, I ended up with a lot of pulled muscles, twisted ankles, & jammed fingers. Connecting with the Earth & praying to Apollo for healing got me through a lot of gym classes without having to pack up & hike to the other end of campus to see the secretary. (Our school only had a nurse for 2 hours on Thursday mornings.)

At some point, he swapped out with his brother Hermes. Neither of them bothered to tell me about it, choosing instead to wait & see how long it took me to figure it out. (Answer: It was YEARS!)
Further information
Name Apollo, Phoebus
Pantheon Greco-Roman
Bailiwick Light, sunlight, music, poetry, prophecy, archery, healing, averting plague & harm
Signs & Symbols Sun, bow & arrow, laurel wreath, lyre, swan, raven, laurel, larkspur, cypress
Preferred Offerings/Tributes Candle devotion, laurel, larkspur, cypress, raven representation, swan representation, music, poems, doing divination, acts of service

Hekate

Thanks to Buffy the Vampire Slayer, I discovered a strong connection with Hekate, Goddess of Witches. Over the years since then, that connection has grown & deepened. Fulfilling my need for a strong, healthy maternal influence, Hekate got me through some of the hardest years of my life.

Name Hekate/Hecate, I call Her "Grandmother"
Pantheon Greco-Roman, probably predates both
Bailiwick Magic, Shadow Work, caring for the forgotten, Ghosts, Witchcraft, Crossroads, Necromancy
Signs & Symbols New Moon, triple moon, keys, torches, dogs, polecat

Hermes

I'm honestly not sure when I started working with Hermes, thanks to his & Apollo's little prank. Although, it IS incredibly "on-brand" for both of them. What finally tipped me off was that Hermes didn't react to a long-running jibe I had with Apollo. Any time Apollo expressed displeasure with something, I'd joke that I was sure he'd rather I wasn't "a Cassandra" (lie to him about my motives or intentions). The day he didn't react to it, I knew something was up. It still took me a little time to figure out it was Hermes answering me, though.
